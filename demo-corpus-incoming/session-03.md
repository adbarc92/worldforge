# Session 3 — The Harvest-Tide Murder

**Date played:** real-world Thursday
**In-world date:** 14th of Harvest-Tide, 847
**Location:** Hollowford, the ducal apartments
**Party:** Corrin, Vesh, Sister Maren, Halden, Old Tomas

## Recap

The party arrived in Hollowford two days ahead of the Harvest-Tide procession, bearing the sealed letter from Thornwatch. Their intended audience was with Duke Aldric personally — the letter's contents, per Vesh's reading, would not land well with a steward.

They were escorted to the ducal apartments on the evening of the 14th and told to wait in the east antechamber.

## The Discovery

Corrin grew impatient after the second bell and slipped past the guard rotation into the private corridor (Stealth 22, guard was eating). He found the interior door to the duke's study ajar.

Inside:

- Duke Aldric was slumped at his writing desk. Throat cut. Still warm.
- The sealed letter the party had brought was open on the desk — read, but the wax seal had not been broken by force. Someone had heated it.
- A silver pendant, Fenland make, was on the floor half under the desk.
- The window latch was open from the inside.

**GM note for the wiki:** Duke Aldric was murdered in his chambers during the harvest festival. This is the inciting event of the arc. The official record will eventually claim a different cause — the regent's office is going to bury this — but the party saw the body.

## What the Party Did

- Vesh pocketed the pendant before the guard returned (unseen).
- Sister Maren performed last rites under her breath, then called the alarm.
- Halden identified the blade work as a single drawn cut, right-handed, by someone the duke had let stand behind him.
- The party were held for questioning for four hours, then released on the intercession of Lady Elayne's chamberlain, who had been expecting them.

## Consequences for Next Session

- The regent's office will announce a cause of death within the week. Whatever they say, the party knows it's a lie.
- The Fenland pendant is a plant or a genuine clue; roll when inspected in detail.
- Lady Elayne has requested a private audience at the Greystone estate. She knows her father was murdered and she knows the party knows.
- The sealed letter from Thornwatch — the one Duke Aldric had clearly just read — is now missing. Someone removed it in the four hours the party spent in the guardroom.

## Loose Threads

- Who let the killer into the corridor?
- The window latch was open from the inside — that means the duke opened it. For whom?
- Why was the pendant left behind? Carelessness or misdirection?
