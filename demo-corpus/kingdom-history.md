# A Brief History of Eastmarch

## The Founding (c. 612–640)

Eastmarch was consolidated under King Othred the Steady in 612, following two decades of skirmishes along the Silverflow. The kingdom's early charter — preserved in the Hollowford archives — established the five great duchies: Westvale, Northreach, Fenmarch, Stormgate, and the Duchy of the Isles.

House Vareth was granted Westvale in 618 for its cavalry's role at the Battle of Silverflow Crossing. The grant is hereditary, with final confirmation of each new duke requiring the assent of the Council of Stewards.

## The Long Peace (640–790)

For nearly a century and a half, Eastmarch enjoyed what later chroniclers called the Long Peace. Trade along the Silverflow flourished, the southern passes to the Fenlands were formalized by treaty, and the court at Hollowford grew into one of the great scholarly centers of the northern kingdoms.

Royal succession during this period was uncontested. Three generations of House Othred held the throne without challenge, and the great houses of the duchies — Vareth, Dunmere, Ashcrest, and Tarrow — largely governed themselves.

## The Fenland Incursions (790–834)

Relations with the Fenlands soured after the death of Queen Marrow in 790. For the next forty-four years, raiding parties crossed the southern marshes each spring. Westvale, bordering the disputed territory, bore the brunt. Duke Halvan Vareth — Aldric's grandfather — held the line at Thornwatch for eleven seasons.

The incursions ended by treaty at Greystone Ford in 834.

## The Present Reign (834–847)

King Aelwin II took the throne in 834 at age twenty-two, promising a restoration of the old order. His early reign was marked by a flurry of appointments and the recall of several aging dukes to the capital.

Duke Aldric Vareth of Westvale inherited his title in 838 upon the death of his father, Duke Halvan. He was known for administrative caution, a lifelong interest in falconry, and his patronage of the mendicant orders that serve the southern villages.

Duke Aldric succumbed to fever in the spring of 847, six months before the harvest festival. The duchy currently sits in stewardship pending the council's confirmation of his heir, Lady Elayne Vareth, his elder daughter by his second marriage.

## Succession Notes

Per the Westvale charter, stewardship must not exceed two years. The council has until the spring of 849 to either confirm Lady Elayne or petition the crown for an alternative claimant. No petition has been filed as of this writing.
