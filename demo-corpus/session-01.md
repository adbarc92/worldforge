# Session 1 — The Letter at Thornwatch

**Date played:** real-world two Thursdays ago
**In-world date:** 2nd of Harvest-Tide, 847
**Location:** Thornwatch garrison, southern Westvale
**Party:** Corrin, Vesh, Sister Maren, Halden, Old Tomas

## Setup

The party had been retained as nominal couriers for a small caravan out of Halebrook, ostensibly carrying tithe records for the mendicant mother house. The real purpose — disclosed only to Sister Maren at the time — was to reach Thornwatch before the monthly dispatch rider and deliver a sealed letter directly into the hand of Captain Varra Dunmere, not through the usual channels.

## What Happened

- The caravan arrived at Thornwatch on the morning of the 2nd. Inspection at the gate took longer than usual. The party noticed a second, unfamiliar check: a Fenland-dressed observer standing behind the gate-sergeant, saying nothing.
- Captain Dunmere met Sister Maren privately in the chapel annex. She read the letter, grew visibly pale, then copied it onto a fresh sheet in her own hand and sealed it with the duchy seal under her authority.
- She handed the copy to Sister Maren with new instructions: deliver this to Duke Aldric personally at Hollowford, not through his office. The original letter she burned.
- On the ride out, Vesh tracked a rider who had departed Thornwatch thirty minutes ahead of the party, heading north on the Hollowford road.

## Combat

Brief skirmish at the Redbrook crossing late afternoon — four roadsmen, not organized, probably opportunists drawn by the small caravan. Old Tomas took a shallow cut on the forearm. Otherwise uneventful.

## Loot

- Twelve silvers off the roadsmen. Split five ways with one share held for the caravan master.
- The sealed copy of the Thornwatch letter (unopened by the party).

## Loose Threads

- Who was the Fenland-dressed observer at Thornwatch gate?
- Who was the rider thirty minutes ahead of the party?
- What does the letter say?
