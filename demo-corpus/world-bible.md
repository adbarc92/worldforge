# Eastmarch — World Bible

A reference for the broad shape of the setting. Keep this lean; specifics belong in the history and faction docs.

## Geography

Eastmarch occupies the basin of the Silverflow, a wide, slow river that flows southwest from the Cold Peaks to the Isles. Five duchies ring the river:

- **Westvale** — agricultural, the breadbasket, ruled by House Vareth from the seat at Vareth-Hold. Southern edge borders the Fenlands.
- **Northreach** — highlands, timber and iron, House Dunmere.
- **Fenmarch** — marshland along the upper Silverflow, mixed loyalties, House Ashcrest.
- **Stormgate** — coastal, shipbuilding, House Tarrow.
- **Duchy of the Isles** — archipelago, mercantile, ruled by a triumvirate rather than a single house.

The capital, Hollowford, sits at the confluence of the Silverflow and the Redbrook and is technically a chartered city independent of any duchy. In practice the crown runs it directly.

## Calendar

The year is divided into eight festival-seasons, each six weeks. The major festivals used by the party:

- **First-Light** (late winter) — new year's rites, traditionally the day royal appointments are announced.
- **Green-Tide** (spring) — sowing, the season of Duke Aldric's death.
- **Midsummer** (high summer) — court season, tournaments.
- **Harvest-Tide** (autumn) — harvest festival, the duke's customary return to Hollowford, the season in which the current arc is set.
- **Long-Dark** (winter) — quiet season, closed roads.

Year-reckoning counts from the founding charter. The current year is 847.

## Currency

Crown coin:
- Copper dram (cp)
- Silver mark (10 drams)
- Gold crown (10 marks)

Fenland coin is accepted at a rough 1:1 silver equivalence but is examined closely at the borders.

## Magic

Magic is rare, slow, and mostly clerical. The mendicant orders perform minor healing and rites of passage but do not fling fire. There is one recognized order of hedge-mages (the Silvercloaks) who answer to the crown. Magic in private hands is legal but closely watched.

No resurrection. Not even rumored. The dead stay dead.

## Tone

Low-to-mid fantasy. Political intrigue, long travel, small skirmishes. The party are competent but not legendary. The setting takes the word "consequence" seriously.

## House Rules Note (for the GM)

- No PC resurrection, per the setting.
- Downtime between sessions advances time at the rate of one in-world day per real-world hour of downtime narration.
- Important NPC schedules are tracked in the appointment log; players can investigate to retrieve them.
