# A Brief History of Aegis Station

Aegis Station's construction was authorized by the Terran Commonwealth's Science Directorate in 2385, two years after the completion of the Lyran Treaty that ended the border skirmishes with the Hegemony. The treaty opened the outer systems to civilian research vessels, and Aegis was conceived as the primary waystation for the return leg of the Lyran expeditions.

Construction began at the Hestia shipyards in 2385 and was completed in 2387. The station was ferried to the Kepler-186 system by the deep-space tug *Orestes* and assembled on station across eleven months. First crew boarded in late 2388.

For its first thirteen years, Aegis served its intended function: a quiet refueling and resupply point for expeditions returning from Lyran space. The discovery of the Halion anomaly by probe drops in 2401 changed the station's character dramatically. Within six months, the Science Directorate had repurposed three of Aegis's laboratories for dedicated anomaly research, and within a year the crew had grown by 60%.

Commander Yusef Alvarado held directorship from 2388 through 2409. He oversaw both the original waystation era and the early anomaly years. His successor, Commander Vale, was appointed following Alvarado's retirement. Under Vale's tenure, Commonwealth Defense has taken an increasingly visible role in station affairs.

The station has never experienced a fatal accident. It has experienced two major reactor scrams (2392 and 2407) and one station-wide power interruption (2405, traced to a faulty coupling in the central bus).
