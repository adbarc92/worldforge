# Excerpts from Archivist Marr's Recovered Logs

**Log 4412-A, dated 2411.09.14.**

Spent the afternoon cleaning up the metadata on the founding-era records. The official history lists Aegis's construction as complete in 2387, but the original commissioning manifests I pulled from the sealed archive clearly date to 2384. Three years' discrepancy. I can't tell yet whether this is a clerical error propagated through the official timeline or something deliberate.

What I can say with confidence: the station's first crew rotation arrived in 2385, not 2388. The records are internally consistent — transport logs from the *Orestes*, personnel transfer orders, even receipts for the first water-reclamation cartridges all point to the earlier date. Whoever wrote the public history used a different set of numbers.

---

**Log 4412-C, dated 2411.10.02.**

Asked XO Koval about the date discrepancy offhand over breakfast. She didn't meet my eyes. Said she'd "look into it" and changed the subject. That's the third time this week someone has done that when I've raised founding-era records. I'm going to stop asking.

The more concerning piece: the original crew manifest names Commander Alvarado's predecessor — someone called *Rett Vaskel* — as the station's first director. There is no Rett Vaskel in the public record. No entry in the Commonwealth personnel database. No trace in any publication I can find. Alvarado is listed as the founding director in every official source.

I don't know what happened between 2384 and 2388, but something did, and it's been papered over thoroughly.
