# Aegis Station

Aegis Station is a Class-IV deep-space research platform orbiting the gas giant Halion in the outer reaches of the Kepler-186 system. Constructed in 2387 by the Terran Commonwealth, the station was originally designed as a waystation for vessels returning from the Lyran expeditions but was repurposed in 2401 to study the so-called "Halion anomaly" — a recurring electromagnetic pulse that emerges from the planet's upper atmosphere every 47 hours.

The station consists of three concentric rings connected by six radial spokes. The outer ring houses crew quarters and life support; the middle ring contains research laboratories and the central computing core; and the inner ring, closest to the main reactor, holds the observation decks and the restricted archives. A total of 214 personnel can be supported indefinitely, though the current complement sits at 187.

Aegis is self-sufficient in most respects. Its hydroponic bays produce 92% of the station's caloric needs, and water is reclaimed from atmospheric condensate at a rate of 99.3%. The station runs on a single fusion reactor with a rated output of 1.8 terawatts, with two backup reactors held in reserve. Commander Iresh Vale has held the post of station director since 2409.

The station is officially listed as a civilian research outpost, though three of its laboratories operate under Commonwealth Defense classifications. The existence of these labs is acknowledged publicly; their research is not.
